UCLA Extension

Machine Translation short course: Syllabus

April 2 (Eduard Hovy)

Introduction

  • What is MT? Who needs it? Why?
  • What makes MT hard?
  • History of MT

Theoretical Foundations

  • Configurations: assimilation/dissemination, pre/in/postediting, etc.
  • Kinds of MT: direct, transfer, IL
  • Knowledge requirements of MT

April 3 (Kevin Knight)

General-purpose MT

  • Statistical MT
  • Combining knowledge-based and statistical methods
  • Automated knowledge acquisition

Markets

  • Evaluating MT systems
  • Current systems, niches, "consumer report" on selected MT systems
  • Hands-on demos

April 4 (Jaime Carbonell)

New MT Paradigms

  • Specialized Knowledge-Based MT
      Case study: KANT (R&D to deployment)
  • Multi-Engine Rapid-deployment MT
      Case study: DIPLOMAT (EBMT, transfer, KBMT...)

Future of MT

  • Speech-to-speech MT
      Case study: JANUS (and international CSTAR consortium)
  • New patterns of use (Web, email, translingual IR, summarization)

Concluding panel (Carbonell, Knight, Hovy)
